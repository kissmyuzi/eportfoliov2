What I aim to learn from the internship

As a current second year student studying engineering, I intend to acquire valuable industry insights from professional engineering teams. I am also looking forward to getting hands-on experience in detection, mitigate and secure infrastructure.

Another area of focus would be communication, which entails the ability to communicate information correctly, succinctly and without obstruction. The emerging international landscape means a diverse workforce from a multitude of background. Gender, Religion and language are all barriers that must be broken for the success of engineering projects. “Groups are particularly good at combining talents and providing innovative solutions to possibly unfamiliar problems;” (Blair, G.1991, p. 219)

As a student majoring in software Engineering but has a strong interest in Cyber security, I aim to work in a role related to either my major or Cyber Security.

My preparation for workplace learning

Throughout the summer semester I took two subjects: Engineering Practice Preparation 1 (EPP1) and Cyber Security(An Offensive Mindset). EPP1 was an interesting subject that focused on maximising our potential during the internship process, while “Cyber Security: An Offensive Mindset” polished my technical and industry knowledge. Since I have written about “An Offensive Mindset” quite extensively in my journals, I will dedicate this topic to EPP1.

EPP1 Subject Description

This subject provides students with the opportunities to collaborate with their peers and the subject’s teaching staff, to explore the social practices involved in seeking, obtaining, contributing to and learning from an engineering job or internship. Students are guided in the curation of an e-portfolio that documents their growing understanding of the practices necessary for seeking and obtaining a professional engineering experience. The e-portfolios also document the student’s growing capabilities to contribute to a workplace, and to engage in workplace learning focusing particularly on the interpersonal and communication capabilities that may be required in engineering workplaces.

EPP1 Subject Learning Outcomes (SLOs)

Upon successful completion of this subject students should be able to:

  1. Articulate preparedness for participating in an engineering workplace
  2. Document and communicate their capabilities to contribute to a workplace and engage in workplace learning

Personally, EPP1 has been a continuous path of self- evaluation and provided valuable feedback towards our e-portfolios. EPP1 first made me realise the importance of social networks in the modern industry, so utilising lecture notes I began refining my LinkedIn profile. Profile photo? Check. Outline? Check. Next, I focused on industry events in which I pushed myself to get as many connections as I can. I started to post about my experience at the industry events, which led to 1000+ views and a sharp increase of profile views by industry professionals.

Another area that I discovered from EPP1 was added advantage of having an E-portfolio. Times are changing and with more and more online applications, an E-portfolio is a great alternative from a traditional CV as it gives the recruiter a more polished overview upon the individual’s skill and experience. During the second week we were tasked to have 3 journals ready for peer review on our E-portfolio. The feedback I received from my peer group was to implement a better navigation system for journal viewing, so I completely recreated my site using Hugo for better aesthetic and navigation system.

Overall, the sessions I have developed myself towards becoming a better employee. I strongly believe that the soft skills and career insights that EPP1 has taught me will aid me in my future workplace.
