Health, Safety and Ethics

Health and Safety

Unlike other engineering counterparts, software engineering is unique in its environment. The workplace of a Software engineer is usually screen based work, which must comply with the Australian Council of Trade Unions(ACTU). The Following are the summarised points of the guideline:

  1. Visual Problems – Computer tasks that reach over two hours per day are deemed visually demanding and thus can cause complications to the eyes.

  2. Musculo - Skeletal Difficulties – Sitting behind a computer for extended periods of time can cause substantial pain and injury to the back, neck and head area.

  3. Stress – Many companies that utilise programs to monitor employees for performance has the potential to create high levels of stress. The latest statistics show that around 18% of all workers experience occupational related stress most of the time.

To mitigate the above conditions, it is recommended for a workplace to undertake relevant controls against health and safety hazards in the workplace:

  1. Elimination – In a computer-based workplace, the first step is to try eliminating the hazard directly. For example: Using non-computer-based methods to complete tasks.

  2. Substitution – When it is elimination is not possible, a safer alternative should be imposed. For example, to reduce eye strain it should be compulsory for all screens to implement blue light reduction software.

  3. Isolation – If both elimination and substitution fail, the next step is to isolate the hazard. Whether if it is placing the hazard in another room or distancing employees, the safety of the employees must be guaranteed.

  4. Engineering Controls - In rare cases where the combination of elimination, substitution and isolation fails, engineering controls could be placed in an environment to mitigate the problem. For example, by re- phasing high-voltage transmission lines it is possible to limit the level of 50 hertz electromagnetic fields in a working environment.

Ethics

For the Software engineering side, I must follow the code of ethics from the “Code of Ethics” from Engineers Australia. Further information could be found in Engineers Australia link in the references.

However, due to my interest in cyber security I am willing to follow the ethics of White Hat Hacking. Which means that I will always comply with local and international laws when testing for vulnerabilities and reporting it to the original vendor. Thus, benefitting the day to day users of those systems and to ultimately build a more secure infrastructure.
