SUMMER STUDIO: SPRINT 4

Reverse Engineering workshop (SLO 3)

On Monday, Ruben, an Application Security manager came to teach us the tools of the trade in Reverse Engineering. Although I have personally touched on this subject area before,but we went straight into the deep-end through experimenting hands-on with Ruben’s Reverse Engineering challenges. The tool we used was Binary Ninja, GNU Debugger and X86 Opcode.

^Ruben helping around the studio.

Overall, the workshop was very challenging as we had very little training before, on the otherhand, everyone of us learnt alot especially the mindset when reversing a program.

SPRINT 4 REFLECTION (SLO 5)

The focus of this week was to have a successful closure of the summer studio, with Monday occupied by a Reverse Engineering workshop by Ruben Thijssen. With the end of the studio near, our final task was to compile our E-portfolio for submission. This took a significant amount of time this week, however, the Reverse Engineering workshop on Monday was extremely interesting as the manager of Application security from Symantec was the one who taught us by hand.

SUBJECT LEARNING OUTCOMES(SLOs)

There are 5 major subject learning outcomes with this course and the achievement of these outcomes will be outlined by this portfolio. The SLO’s are:

  1. Engage with Stakeholders to identify a problem (empathizing/ defining the problem)
  2. Apply Design Thinking to respond to a defined or newly identified problem
  3. Apply Technical skills to develop, model and/or evaluate design (refining/ test a prototype)
  4. Demonstrate effective collaboration and communication
  5. Conduct critical self and peer review and performance evaluation (both as an individual and a team)
